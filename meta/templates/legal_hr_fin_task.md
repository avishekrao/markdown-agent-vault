---

## Option A: Lite (fill in 3-5 minutes)

### 1. For whom and what problem
Who suffers from the problem (role/division/category of employees or customers) and what is the pain.

### 2. How to solve now and why bad
The current process, its weaknesses, risks and losses.

### 3. What do we suggest we do?
In short: what solution we implement (documents, rules, process, control).

### 4. What will affect
What commands/systems/processes will change?

### 5. Access and where data
What systems the role has access to and where sensitive data is stored.

### 6. When the task is completed (Definition of Done)
3-6 check items "made / not done".

### 7. How do we know it worked?
1-3 signs of success (metric or observed effect)

---

## Option B: Full (for critical changes)

### 1. Context and user
- `User/customer`: <who initiates >>
- `Who cares?`: <what roles/groups >>
- `Scale`: <How many people/processes >>

### 2. Problem.
- Which doesn't work.
- What is the risk (legal, financial, operational, reputational)?

### 3. Current way
- Like we do now.
- Why is it unsustainable/expensive/unmanageable?

### 4. Proposed decision
- What do we do?
  - documents;
  - Changes in regulations/LNA;
  - changes in the process;
  - Control and responsible.
- What we do not do (the boundaries of the task).

### 5. Processes and systems affected
- Systems: <CRM / task tracker / mail / documents / ...>
- Processes: <onboarding / dismissal / payments / access / document management >>
- Divisions: <Legal / HR / Finance / IT / Sales >>

### 5.1. Access and confidential data
- What data is available to the target roles.
- Where this data lives (CRM, analytics, wiki, docs, mail, instant messengers, etc.).
- Where is the maximum risk of leakage now?

### 6. Artifacts of the result
- What documents / templates / checklists should appear?
- Where they're stored.
- Who owns them?

### 7. Definition of Done
- [ ] A package of documents has been approved.
- [ ] Signed with a task force of current employees.
- [ ] Added to the mandatory package for new employees.
- [ ] The control process was started and the owner was appointed.
- [ ] There is a working checklist of execution (for example, a digital workaround list).

### 8. Criteria for success
- Procedural: <proportion of cases executed under the new regulations>.
- Risk: Reducing incidents/violations.
- Operational: "speed and completeness of closing cases".

### 9. Risk and dependency
- Which could block the introduction.
- from whom decisions/consents are needed.

### 10. Participants in the process and areas of responsibility
- Who initiates.
- Who agrees (Legal/HR/Finance/IT/Sec)
- Who controls the execution (main and reserve).

### 11. First step.
The smallest action after which the task actually starts (for example, "gather access map by role").

---

## What we leave from the product template and what we adapt
- Leave it:
  - "Who's the user?"
  - "What's the problem?"
  - "How do you decide now?"
  - "How do we propose to decide?"
  - "What will it do?"
  - "How do we measure success?"
- Adapt:
  - instead of features and story points: processes, documents, execution control;
  - Instead of deadlines: priority `critical/normal/minor` + the obvious next step.
  - We need to add `Definition of Done`.
- Normally you don't.
  - Detailing "how many users" with high accuracy, if this does not change the decision;
  - complex product metrics if the compliance/legal task and effect is verified through compliance with the process.

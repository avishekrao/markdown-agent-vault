# Lists of stop words by Ilyakhov

Handbook for the editor. Each word in these lists is a candidate for removal or replacement.
But remember, a stop word is a word that makes no sense in a particular context.
In another context, the same word may be appropriate.

---

## 1. Introduction structures

Introductory words often add nothing to the meaning. They create the illusion of politeness or learning, but they actually inhibit reading. Remove them if the sentence without them does not lose meaning.

A special case – references to “well-known” (“everybody knows”, “not a secret”, “as you know”). If the fact is really familiar to everyone, there is no need to write about it. If you talk about something new, but present it as common knowledge, it is not true. Replace with a specific reference to the source.

Brackets and clarifications (“more precisely”, “more correctly”, “in other words”) are a tracing from oral speech. In the letter, state it exactly. Information from brackets is either deleted or taken out and made a full part of the sentence.

## 2. Uncertainty

Undefined pronouns ("some", "some", "some", "some") and the words "different", "different" - delete or replace with accurate information. Exception: if the essence of the situation is in the unknown (“Someone gave the client our prices – we will investigate”).

A year ago, a company called Horns and Hoofs filed for bankruptcy.
**OK: ** A year ago, Horns and Hoofs filed for bankruptcy.

I got this information from a local newspaper.
**I read about it in the local newspaper.

## 3. Imposed assessments

Subjective words by which the author tries to force the reader to draw a conclusion without evidence. They create a false sense of work done. Replace it with facts, stories or demonstrations.

The same group includes amplifiers (“maximum”, “very”, “absolutely”, “perfectly”) – they make the estimates even less convincing.

**Bad:** Quick earnings → **Good:** We pay 900 rubles per hour, payments every day
**Bad:** "Unforgettable atmosphere of warmth and comfort" → **Good:** "Come to us for breakfast, gather with friends, book a room for your birthday"
**Bad:** The performance is very long → **Good:** The performance lasts 4.5 hours

## 6. Stamps.

Common phrases that were once fresh, but from widespread use lost their power. Replace specifics with facts, scenarios, evidence.

**Bad: ** We have a real team of professionals in our state.

## 7-step Hero protocol (short version)

1. **Stage Schwartz.** Determine the Traffic Awareness Stage:
   Unknown / problem / solution / product / most. One Hero at all stages. By default.
   B2B SaaS is a solution-aware solution.
2. ** Positioning core (Trout/Ries) ** One sentence:
   “[Product] is a [category] for [who] is [benefit], unlike [alternative].
   because [differentiator].” Lacmus: set up a competitor, does that sound? If so, copy it.
3. **10 candidates H1.** Different range: PAS/AIDA, active verb, triad, contrast, number,
   Brand-ownable provocation. The goal is range, not "one masterpiece."
4. ** Run through 5 filters.** Visualize / Falsify / Brand-ownable / Rhythm + clarity / Target-language filter.
   There are 3 finalists out of 10.
5. **Subhead.** 1-2 lines, 16-25 words in the target language. Does not repeat H1 - carries a new quantum:
   proof, benefit, withdrawal of objection. Specifics are mandatory.
6. H1 + subhead + proof + primary CTA + visual hint. One primary CTA.
   CTA - verb + object, 2-4 words. Proof is specific (digit/name/KPI).
7. ** Validation checklist.** 5-second test, three-question test (Harry Dry), Trout/Ries litmus,
   message-match, RU-filter.

## Schwartz: 5 Stages and What to Say

| Stage | What Hero knows about |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Unaware | Not aware of the problem | Context, hook, provocation (NOT product) |
| Problem-aware | Knows the problem, not the solution | Problem + Promise of solution |
| Solution-aware | Knows solutions, not your product | Benefits + why your type of solution is better |
| Product-aware | Knows the product, not sure | Differentiator, proof, removal of objections |
| Most-aware | Wants to buy | Offer, urgency, CTA|

## Ogilvy headline rules (applicable)

- Indicate the audience in the title (role/industry/size).
- No puns, allusions, word games in B2B without an awareness budget.
- Numbers > generic words.
- The title should be self-contained without body copy.
- An active verb is better than a noun.

---

## Harry Dry — three-question test

1. **Visualize: * Can you see the picture in your head in 1 second?
2. **Falsify:** Verified as true/false?
3. **Can a competitor say the same thing?

The three "yes/yes/no" headline is strong.

## Target-language clarity filter

**Stop words and tracings that will fail any Hero:**
- Solutions (as synonymous with products)
- optimization
- seamless, seamless integration
- ecosystem
- innovative, innovative platform
- unique, effective, high-quality
- integrated approach
- leading

**Rules:
- Verbs > Communions (“We pay” > “Making payments”).
- Specifics > Evaluation ("3 minutes" > "quick").
- One thought = one sentence.
- In target-language hero works well 5-9 words.
- An active verb in the beginning is a powerful technique.

## Formulas are cheat sheets

**PAS (Problem → Agitation → Solution).** For unknown/problem-aware. More emotional than AIDA.

*AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action) ** For hot traffic in buying mode.

**4U (Urgent, Unique, Useful, Ultra-specific).** Checklist for a separate phrase, not structure.

## Techniques of “calling” (without loss of clarity)

- ** Triad:** Advertising. Fees. Reports.
- “Grows with you, not at your expense.”
- **Pause through dash/point:** One click. Forget ten contracts.”
- **Specific metonymy: ** One act instead of document facilitation.
- **Active verb at the beginning: * Pay in one act.
- **Number instead of adjective: * 4 times faster.

---

## Principles that are not violated

- **Clarity > cleverness** in B2B SaaS with no awareness budget (~85-90% A/B cases).
- **Brand-ownable - mandatory filter.**Trout/Ries Lacmus passes every candidate.
- **One Hero = one quantum of meaning.** Several value props = several landing pages.
- The Schwartz stage is a solution, not a theory. You write blindly without her.
- **The target language is not a word-for-word copy of English.** Calques make the text weaker and less specific.

---

## Minimum user input

Without these 4 Hero items, you can not write - ask a clarifying question:

1. What kind of product (1-2 sentences).
2. ICP / role / industry.
3. Source of traffic (or at least the Schwartz stage)
4. 1-2 differentiators from alternatives.
